04 / Objection handling

Five objections in every enterprise deal

Objection

We've invested heavily in our WMS. Replacing it would cost time and money.

Reframe

StackBOX integrates with your existing WMS as the orchestration layer. You keep your warehouse execution investment and add order and transport coordination on top. Godrej ran this hybrid model for integration without full cutover risk.

Objection

How is this different from Locus or FarEye? They're more established.

Position

Locus and FarEye optimize last-mile transport. StackBOX optimizes warehouse-to-transport coordination. You need both layers. We focus on the orchestration layer they don't solve.

Objection

We've had SaaS implementation failures before. What's your deployment track record?

Approach

We pilot early with defined ROI gates before full commitment. Customers evaluate the coordination layer in production before scaling. This reduces deployment risk and aligns implementation to business impact.

Objection

Your pricing model needs to fit our volume and growth plans.

Solution

Enterprise contracts support per-location, per-order, or hybrid models. We build pricing that mirrors your cost structure. Existing customers range from predictable stable volume to seasonal spike patterns.

Objection

We operate across Southeast Asia. Is this India-only?

Roadmap

Proven in India at scale. Platform is cloud-native and multi-region ready. Carrier integrations and compliance vary by market, but core orchestration logic operates the same across geographies.

09 / Sales call takeaways

How to refine positioning in the field

PMM doesn't end at launch. It gets sharper in sales conversations. If I joined StackBOX, these are the three questions I'd track after every enterprise pitch:

Which objection dominates?

Track whether legacy system integration or competitor comparison comes up more often. Adjust messaging priority based on actual deal patterns, not assumptions.

Which buyer persona is most skeptical?

Do CTOs push back on architecture claims? Do CFOs challenge ROI timelines? Do Ops directors want more benchmarking? Tailor proof points to the hardest-to-convince buyer.

Where does positioning break?

When prospects say "but Locus does X better," flag it. That's a real gap in positioning. Update battlecards monthly with patterns from sales calls.
